PerM: Tool for Perception-based Runtime Monitoring for Human-Construction Robot Systems

The rising use of robots in construction aims to ease labor-intensive and hazardous tasks. Ensuring safety in human-robot collaboration at construction sites is crucial, necessitating robust safety protocols and smooth interaction. This work aims to create a free open-source tool, PerM, for monitoring safety in dynamic cyber-physical systems with robots in construction scenarios. Our framework detects, localizes, and predicts human motion in the robot's view, assessing future human/robot safety specified using signal temporal logic (STL) specifications. Real-time data from an RGB-D camera identifies deviations from expected behavior. Extensive simulations and case studies on a realistic construction site validate our approach. Our experiments show that simple safety rules for robots working in unstructured, fast-changing environments like construction sites do not work in practice, e.g., a robot may stop and wait forever for human workers to move safely; hence, it cannot finish its task. .
