Vision-based Runtime Monitoring for Human-Construction Robot Systems

Robots have been increasingly used in construction sites to reduce the human workers' workload on labor-intensive and dangerous tasks. Human-construction robots collaboration on a construction site creates a unique safety-critical Cyber-Physical System, which requires new techniques to monitor the safety of human workers at runtime. Existing monitoring techniques analyze the system behaviors against its specifications using simulated or recorded system states defined based on a global coordination over a bounded time period in a centralized manner. However, in the real world, such global view usually does not exist in practice, preventing the applicability of current monitoring techniques to real-world applications. In this paper, we propose a monitoring approach that uses only the robot perception to monitor the safety of human workers at runtime under the robot local view. Our approach uses a YOLO network to detect a human worker and then performs clustering and Kalman filtering for point-cloud data from a depth camera to estimate the relative human motion under the robot's local coordination. The estimated human states are used to monitor the potential collision between the human and the robot at runtime. We evaluate our approach using simulation. The experiment results show that our approach is promising for real-time safety monitoring of real-world human-robot collaboration applications.
